Liveaboard Phinisi — 3 Days, 2 Nights

You sleep on the boat. You wake up between islands, in silence, with nothing between you and the horizon. At dawn, manta rays glide beneath the hull. By 6 AM you are hiking Padar before any other tourist has arrived. This is not a tour. It is a way of being in the world for 72 hours that most people never find.

Day 1
Departure · Kelor Island · Kalong Island
Board in Labuan Bajo harbour. Sail to Kelor for snorkeling and panoramic views. Arrive Kalong at dusk to witness thousands of fruit bats take flight. Sleep at anchor under open sky.
Day 2
Padar at Dawn · Pink Beach · Komodo Dragons · Manta Point
Pre-sunrise hike to Padar summit — completely alone. Snorkel at Pink Beach. Trek to see Komodo dragons in their natural habitat. Swim with manta rays at Manta Point — the moment most guests call life-changing.
Day 3
Taka Makassar · Kanawa Island · Return
Morning snorkel at the empty white sandbar of Taka Makassar. Final swim at Kanawa's pristine reef. Return to Labuan Bajo by afternoon — changed.

What nobody tells you

The insider's
Komodo truth.

After hundreds of trips on these waters, we have learned things no travel blog will tell you. Here is what actually separates a good trip from the one you will tell your grandchildren about.

🦅
Padar: 5:30 AM, Not 9 AM
The summit gets crowded by 9. We hike at 5:30 — completely alone, with extraordinary light. Most operators miss this window entirely.
🐠
Manta Rays: Tide is Everything
Mantas feed at slack tide — a 90-minute window. Miss it and you see nothing. We know exactly when that window is, every single day.
🦎
Dragons Are Genuinely Wild
Komodo dragons are ambush predators. Our rangers know how to read their behaviour. This is not something to compromise on.
🌊
The Currents Are Serious
Some of Asia's strongest currents run through Komodo. Our guides know every pattern. Where you enter the water matters enormously.
🌧️
Rainy Season = Manta Season
November–March brings fewer tourists, calmer mornings, and the best manta ray sightings of the year. A hidden gem window.
📵
No Signal = Total Reset
Once you leave harbour, there is no internet. No emails. For most guests, this turns out to be the best part of the entire trip.
